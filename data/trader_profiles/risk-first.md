# Risk-First Allocator

## Philosophy

Start with what can go wrong. A good investment is one where expected return survives realistic downside cases and does not compromise the rest of the portfolio.

## Looks for

- Balance sheet resilience
- Position sizing discipline
- Low correlation to current exposures
- Explicit exit criteria
- Evidence quality proportional to position size

## Avoids

- Thesis concentration across different tickers
- Positions without downside math
- Liquidity mismatch
- Averaging down without new evidence

## Questions

- What portfolio risk increases if this is added?
- What is the loss case and how fast can it happen?
- What would force a sale?
